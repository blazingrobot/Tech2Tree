Tech2Trees Initiative Spurs Surge in Tree Planting and E-Waste Recycling Across Davao

A wave of environmental action is sweeping across Davao City as the Tech2Trees initiative continues to gain momentum, inspiring communities to recycle e-waste in exchange for planting trees. Since its launch, the website—designed to promote responsible disposal of electronic waste—has led to a dramatic increase in both tree planting and recycling efforts, with thousands of mangrove trees now rooted along the city’s coastline. In a recent coastal restoration event, over 3,000 mangrove saplings were planted by volunteers from ASEZ WAO, a global youth group known for its climate action campaigns. Participants donned light blue and yellow vests as they worked together to rehabilitate the shoreline, reinforcing the city’s natural defenses against erosion and storm surges. This event marks one of many community-led efforts fueled by Tech2Trees, a platform that allows users to dispose of old phones, laptops, and other electronics in exchange for tree planting credits. According to organizers, the program has already facilitated the recycling of over 5,000 kilograms of e-waste since its inception, with each device contributing to the planting of up to 20 trees, depending on its condition and recyclability. “The response has been overwhelming,” said Tech2Trees coordinator Maria Liza Fernandez. “People are not only decluttering their homes—they’re actively restoring ecosystems. It’s a win-win for both the environment and the community.” The initiative has partnered with local recyclers and tree-planting organizations to ensure transparency and impact. Users can track their contributions through the website, viewing updates on where their trees are planted and how their recycled devices are processed. Environmental groups like IDIS (Interfacing Development Interventions for Sustainability) have praised the program for bridging digital waste management with climate resilience. “Tech2Trees is a model for how tech and sustainability can work hand in hand,” said IDIS spokesperson Rafael Cruz. As Davao continues to face challenges from climate change and urban waste, programs like Tech2Trees offer a hopeful path forward—one tree, one device at a time.

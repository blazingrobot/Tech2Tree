UIC’s CCS TECHNOSPHERE

In a bold move to unite technology and sustainability, the University of the Immaculate Conception (UIC) College of Computer Studies is set to host CCS TECHNOSPHERE: A Green Computing Drive for a Sustainable Future on September 17, 2025. The event will take place at the ITRC Training Room, L204 & L205, within the UIC Fr. Selga Campus, and is expected to draw students, educators, and tech advocates from across the region. Designed with a green digital theme, CCS TECHNOSPHERE aims to raise awareness about the environmental impact of modern computing and promote practical solutions for reducing electronic waste and energy consumption. The initiative aligns with several United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), including responsible consumption, climate action, and sustainable innovation. The event will feature interactive sessions, tech exhibits, and workshops focused on eco-friendly computing practices. Topics include energy-efficient programming, proper e-waste disposal, and the role of green IT in shaping a more sustainable digital future. Attendees will also learn how to integrate sustainability into software development, hardware usage, and IT infrastructure planning. Organizers from the College of Computer Studies emphasized the importance of educating future tech professionals on the environmental consequences of their work. “Green computing isn’t just a trend—it’s a responsibility,” said CCS faculty coordinator Mark Anthony Reyes. “Through CCS TECHNOSPHERE, we want our students to see that innovation and sustainability can go hand in hand.” The event also serves as a platform to showcase student-led projects that tackle real-world environmental challenges using technology. From apps that track carbon footprints to systems that optimize energy use in data centers, the projects reflect UIC’s commitment to producing socially responsible graduates. For more information, interested participants can visit fb.com/uic.ccs or contact the organizers via email at ccs@uic.edu.ph.

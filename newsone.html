UIC plastic-free university

The University of the Immaculate Conception (UIC) has been officially recognized as a “Plastic-Free School” by the environmental advocacy group Interfacing Development Interventions for Sustainability (IDIS), in celebration of Plastic-Free July 2024. This distinction highlights UIC’s proactive commitment to environmental sustainability, particularly through its early adoption of a campus-wide “No to Single-Use Plastic Policy.” Implemented well before the passage of Davao City’s own ordinance banning single-use plastics, UIC’s policy reflects the institution’s leadership in promoting eco-conscious practices within the academic community. The initiative was spearheaded by the Office of Student Affairs and Services, under the direction of Anaflor E. Sacopayo, and supported by the University Student Government led by Charles Andre F. Nieves. The recognition from IDIS—a non-profit organization dedicated to watershed protection and sustainable development in South Central Mindanao—places UIC among a select group of schools in Davao City that have taken concrete steps to reduce plastic waste on campus. According to IDIS, these institutions serve as models for how educational spaces can contribute meaningfully to environmental protection, even in the absence of formal legislation. UIC’s efforts include banning plastic straws, utensils, and bags within school premises, encouraging reusable alternatives, and conducting awareness campaigns among students and staff. The university’s early compliance with sustainable practices not only earned praise from environmental groups but also positioned it as a frontrunner in the region’s shift toward greener policies. As environmental challenges continue to mount globally, UIC’s recognition serves as a reminder that meaningful change often begins at the grassroots level—with schools, students, and educators leading the way.
